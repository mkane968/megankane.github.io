About Me

I am a second-year graduate student at Temple University pursuing a PhD English with a concentration in Rhetoric and Composition and certification in Cultural Analytics. I graduated from Elizabethtown College in January 2019 with a BA in English and concentration in Secondary Education. I also have experience in feature writing, grant writing, academic writing, web writing, creative writing and publishing. In my free time, I enjoy reading (of course!), playing the violin, and spending time with family and friends.

Current Research

My research focuses on utilizing computational software to inform composition assessment and pedagogy. My current project explores DocuScope, a computational rhetorical analysis platform developed at Carnegie Mellon University, as a potential tool to inform first-year writing assessment.

Current Industry Projects

Along with pursuing my doctorate, I am passionate about developing high-quality content that engages readers and connects them with vital community programs and services. In the past five years, I have enjoyed crafting print and web content for organizations including Inspired Design Studio, Senior Day Services, Happenings Magazine, The Wright Center for Community Health, and Hippocampus Magazine and Publishing Company.
